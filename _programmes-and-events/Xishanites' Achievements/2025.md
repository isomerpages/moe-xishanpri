<h3><strong>Malay Language</strong></h3>
<h4><strong>Rentas Pantas (Malay Language Amazing Race) – National Level</strong></h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="Rentas Pantas (Malay Language Amazing Race) - National Level" src="/images/rentas_pantas.jpg">
</div>
<p>Our Primary 5 Malay Language students took part in Rentas Pantas 2025,
an Amazing Race-style competition held at the National Institute of Education
(NIE). Organised by the NTU-NIE Malay Language and Cultural Society, the
event offered a series of engaging and meaningful activities that fostered
a deeper appreciation for the Malay language and culture. A standout moment
was the impressive Silat performance, presented in collaboration with Cantonment
Primary School. We are delighted to announce that our students achieved
second place as runners-up, a testament to their talent, teamwork, and
enthusiasm.</p>
<h4><strong>Peraduan TeeVers (Podcast Competition) – National Level</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<p>Muhammad Amsyar from 5 Self-Discipline and Nur Ariana from 5 Integrity
shone brightly at the National TeeVers Competition 2025, emerging as champions!
Representing Xishan Primary School as Rakan Bahasa (Friends of Malay Language),
the duo impressed the judges with their expressive voice acting and a heartfelt
script centred on the theme 'Singapura, Kebanggaanku' (Singapore, My Pride).
Their creativity, confidence, and teamwork not only earned them individual
trophies but also helped secure the school championship trophy, making
this a truly memorable achievement for Xishan Primary.</p>

<h4><strong>Pesta Bahasa Utara (Malay Language Fiesta) – Zonal Level</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="Pesta Bahasa" src="/images/pesta_bahasa.jpg">
</div>
<p>Pesta Bahasa Utara 2025 is an annual Malay Language Fiesta that provides
a platform for students to celebrate and showcase their talents in the
Malay language and culture. Through various cultural competitions, students
develop their language skills, creativity, and confidence while sharing
their love for Malay heritage.</p>
<p>We are proud of our students for their outstanding performances at this
year’s fiesta:</p>
<p>Primary 1 (Folk Song) – Consolation</p>
<p>Primary 2 (Folklore) – 1st Place
<br>Primary 3 (Poetry Singing) – 2nd Place
<br>Primary 4 (Quatrain Skit) – 3rd Place
<br>Primary 5 (Poem Recitation) – 1st Place
<br>Primary 6 (Language Rap) – 1st Place</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>With these stellar results, Xishan also secured overall 1<sup>st</sup> runner-up,
a testament to our students’ hard work, creativity, and teamwork. Their
efforts were recognized with both individual awards and a school trophy,
celebrating their collective achievement.</p>

<h3><strong>Tamil Language</strong></h3>
<p>Our Tamil students participated in various competitions such as the Thirukkural
Vizha and Muthamizh Vizha at the national level, and the Tamil Literary
Competition at the Nee Soon Link constituency level. These contests aimed
to spark students’ interest in spoken Tamil, improve their language skills,
and deepen their understanding of their mother tongue, while building their
confidence in using the language effectively. Our students performed well
and won several prizes.</p>
