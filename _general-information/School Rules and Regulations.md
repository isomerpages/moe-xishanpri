<h1><strong>School Rules and Regulations</strong></h1>
<p>All students are reminded to exercise the school values (Self- discipline,
Integrity, Respect and Resilence) and behave responsibly at all times,
both within and outside school premises. wearing the school uniform, neat
personal appearance and good personal conduct must be maintained at all
times.</p>
<h3>Pledge Taking</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Students who are Singapore citizens must sing the National Anthem and
take the pledge. Students will take the pledge with the right fist clenched
at the heart.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are to wear the prescribed school uniform and modification to
the uniform is <strong><u>NOT</u></strong> allowed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All male students must be clean-shaven at all times. Moustache and beard
are <strong><u>NOT</u></strong> allowed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are not allowed to have in their possession any weapon. They
are also not allowed to bring any weapon-like item which is intended to
be used to cause harm to others.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h3>School Attire</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The prescribed PE attire must be worn for PE lessons and CCAs, and modification
to the attire is strictly <strong><u>NOT</u></strong> allowed. Students are
allowed to wear their PE attire on days they have PE or PAL lessons.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>P5 and P6 students are allowed to wear the school-issued CCA T-shirt every
Thursday.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>P5 and P6 students are allowed to wear the school-issued CCA T-shirt every
Thursday.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All Polo T-Shirt must have the name tag sewn on the left side (above the
school crest) .</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All students must use school approved black shoes and black socks. Decorating
the black shoes with accessories and colours is strictly&nbsp;<strong>NOT</strong>&nbsp;allowed.
The black socks need to be above ankle level.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Facial hair, such as moustache and beard, is&nbsp;&nbsp;<strong>NOT</strong>&nbsp;allowed.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h3>Attendance</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Students are required to attend</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>lessons every day and all assemblies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>all school functions as required</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>remedial/supplementary classes (as informed by teachers)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>respective CCAs</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ol start="2">
<li>
<p>Parents need to inform the school of their child’s absence from school
or any of the activities mentioned above. Upon returning to school, the
student must produce a medical certificate or a letter from the parent/guardian,
stating the reason for absence.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are to be punctual for all activities and must be appropriately
dressed. Pupils must report to school by 7.30 a.m.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h3>General</h3>
<p>1.&nbsp; Students must observe the 3<strong>P</strong>s (<strong>P</strong>unctuality,&nbsp;<strong>P</strong>roper
Attire and&nbsp;<strong>P</strong>roper Behaviour).</p>
<p>2.&nbsp; Students must take good care of the school property and help
to keep the school premises clean and tidy.</p>
<p>3. Students must put the school motto into practice:&nbsp;<strong>LOVE, CARE and SHARE.</strong>
</p>
<p>4. Students are not allowed to bring the following items to school:</p>
<p>•&nbsp;Toys and related electronic items – For example, mobile phone,
MP3 player, IPad, tablet, smart watch etc.</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;Any other items with the potential to cause hurt and injury
to self or others.</p>
<p>5.&nbsp; Students are not allowed to have in their possession any weapon.
Th<strong>ey are also not allowed to bring any weapons-like item which is used or intended to be used to cause harm to others.</strong>
</p>
<p>If the above-mentioned items in paragraph 4 &amp; 5 are found with the
students, they will be confiscated and parents need to come to the school
to collect the item(s) personally.</p>
<p>Should there be a need for students to bring mobile phone/smart watch
to school, parents can go to the General Office to fill up a form on “Request
for Permission to Bring Mobile Phone/Smart Watch To School”. School will
evaluate and grant permission on a case-by-case basis.</p>
<h3>Anti-Bullying Policy</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Bullying is defined as the deliberate and repeated behaviours intended
to cause hurt, distress or humiliation. It can be physical, verbal, social
and/or cyber in nature.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Physical</strong> - persistent in hitting someone or damaging someone
else’s belongings</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Verbal</strong> - repeated name-calling, spreading rumours or making
threats</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Social</strong> - leaving someone out of a group or things on purpose</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Cyber</strong> - using online messages, images or videos to hurt
someone</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ol start="2">
<li>
<p>The school maintains a zero-tolerance approach to bullying in all its
forms. Anchored in our school motto of Love, Care, Share, we strongly believe
that every member of our school community has the right to feel safe, respected,
and valued. No one should be bullied, and no one should engage in bullying
behaviour.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Reporting Procedure:</strong> Students and parents/guardians may
report bullying directly to teachers, school counsellors, or School Leaders.
All reports will be taken seriously and treated confidentially.</p>
</li>
</ol>
