<h1><strong>School Rules and Regulations</strong></h1>
<p>All students are reminded to exercise the school values (Self- discipline,
Integrity, Respect and Resilence) and behave responsibly at all times,
both within and outside school premises. wearing the school uniform, neat
personal appearance and good personal conduct must be maintained at all
times.</p>
<h3>Pledge Taking</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Students who are Singapore citizens must sing the National Anthem and
take the pledge. Students will take the pledge with the right fist clenched
at the heart.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are to wear the prescribed school uniform and modification to
the uniform is <strong><u>NOT</u></strong> allowed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All male students must be clean-shaven at all times. Moustache and beard
are <strong><u>NOT</u></strong> allowed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are not allowed to have in their possession any weapon. They
are also not allowed to bring any weapon-like item which is intended to
be used to cause harm to others.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h3>School Attire</h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The prescribed PE attire must be worn for PE lessons and CCAs, and modification
to the attire is strictly <strong><u>NOT</u></strong> allowed. Students are
allowed to wear their PE attire on days they have PE or PAL lessons.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>P5 and P6 students are allowed to wear the school-issued CCA T-shirt every
Thursday.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All school Polo T-shirts must have the name tag sewn on the left side
(above the school crest).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All students must use school approved black shoes and black socks. Decorating
the black shoes with accessories and colours is strictly NOT allowed. The
black socks need to be above ankle level.</p>
<p></p>
<h3>Attendance</h3>
</li>
</ol>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Students are required to attend</p>
</li>
</ol>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>lessons every day and all assemblies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>all school functions as required</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>remedial/supplementary classes (as informed by teachers) and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>respective CCAs</p>
</li>
</ul>
<ol start="2">
<li>
<p>Parents need to inform the school of their child’s absence from school
or any of the activities mentioned above. Upon returning to school, the
student must produce a medical certificate or a letter from the parent/guardian,
stating the reason for absence.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students’ absence supported by a letter from their parent/guardian with
a valid reason, for example, unwell the day after the period covered by
the medical certificate, external examination for ballet or music, etc.
would be denoted as Valid Reason (Private) [VR(P)]. <strong>The number of days absent due to VR(P) should not exceed 5 per semester.</strong> If
a student exceeds the accepted number of VR(P) per semester, subsequent
absences will be considered invalid and this will affect his/her conduct
grade. Travelling during term time is generally not considered a valid
reason for absence. Parents who wish to take their child overseas for vacation
or to attend to personal matters should do so during the school holidays.
If there is an urgent need to travel during term time, parents are requested
to write in to the school.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are to be punctual for all activities and must be appropriately
dressed. Students must report to school by 7.30 a.m. Students will be considered
as late if they are unable to reach the assembly point outside the canteen
by 7.30 a.m.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h3>General</h3>
<p>1.&nbsp; Students must observe the 3<strong>P</strong>s (<strong>P</strong>unctuality,&nbsp;<strong>P</strong>roper
Attire and&nbsp;<strong>P</strong>roper Behaviour).</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;Students are to take good care of the school property and help
to keep the school premises clean and tidy.</p>
<p>3. Students must put the school motto into practice:&nbsp;<strong>LOVE, CARE and SHARE.</strong>
</p>
<p>4. Students are not allowed to bring the following items to school:&nbsp;</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Toys</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Electronic items – For example, mobile phone, iPad, tablet, smart watch
(except POSB Smart Buddy), etc.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>5.&nbsp;No jewellery or accessories are to be worn in school. Girls are
allowed to wear a pair of simple gold or silver ear rings/ear studs. Accessories
such as ear rings/ear studs and nose rings are<strong> not allowed</strong> for
boys.</p>
<p>If the above-mentioned items in Paragraphs 4 &amp; 5 are found with the
students, they will be confiscated and parents/guardians need to come to
the school to collect the item(s) personally.</p>
<p></p>
<h3>Mobile Phones/Smart Watches Policy</h3>
<p>All students are strongly discouraged from bringing their mobile phones/smart
watches to school. Do note that students can be contacted via the General
Office and students can also use the phone in the General Office to contact
parents/guardians if needed.</p>
<p>The use of mobile phones/smart watches^ will not be allowed during school
hours including recess, CCA and after-school programmes (e.g. supplementary
/ enrichment / remedial lessons / learning journeys). If the mobile phone
is brought to school, the phone must be switched off and kept in the school
bag during curriculum hours and after-school programmes. This is to minimise
disruptions to teaching and learning. Students should bear responsibility
for keeping their mobile phones/smart watches safe. Students are only allowed
to use their mobile phones/smart watches outside the school compound after
school hours.</p>
<p>If the mobile phone/smart watch is (i) not switched off or (ii) not kept
in bag or (iii) used within the school compound:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>First offence: Student would be advised to switch off the mobile phone/smart
watch and place it in their bag and parent/guardian would be notified</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Repeated offences: Mobile phone/smart watch would be confiscated and parent/guardian
would need to come to the school <strong>on the same day</strong> to collect
the mobile phone/smart watch.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Students are allowed to wear POSB Buddy Watches for e-payment. However,
pairing POSB Smart Buddy Tags with devices like my First Fone is not allowed
in schools as myFirstFone has smartwatch-like features, such as messaging
and voice/video calls, which may cause distractions and disrupt learning.</p>
